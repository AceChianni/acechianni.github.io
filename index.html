Designing for real behavior

My work focuses on reducing cognitive load, supporting emotional regulation, and creating systems that adapt to how people actually think, feel, and act.

I’m especially interested in neurodivergent UX, where traditional patterns like streaks, gamification, and dense interfaces often fail. Instead, I design calm, flexible, and low-pressure experiences that are easier to return to — not just start.

How I design

  • Clarity first: strong hierarchy and reduced friction.
  • Accessibility baseline: readable, inclusive patterns.
  • Behavior-aware decisions: grounded in psychology and usability.
  • Build-ready thinking: systems that translate cleanly to development.
