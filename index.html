Designing for real behavior

I design for how people actually behave—not how we expect them to. My work focuses on reducing cognitive load, supporting emotional regulation, and creating systems that make it easier for users to take action.

I’m especially interested in neurodivergent UX, where traditional patterns like streaks, dense dashboards, and open-ended tasks can create friction. Instead, I design calm, flexible experiences that are easier to return to—not just start.

How I design

I approach design as a system of decisions—balancing user needs, business goals, and technical constraints to create usable, scalable products.

  • Clarity first: strong hierarchy and reduced friction.
  • Accessibility baseline: readable, inclusive patterns.
  • Behavior-aware decisions: grounded in psychology and usability.
  • Build-ready thinking: systems that translate cleanly to development.
