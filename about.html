Designing at the intersection of emotion, behavior, and systems

I didn’t arrive here through a linear path, and that’s what shapes how I design. My background spans psychology, film, visual art, and frontend development, all coming together in a practice rooted in emotional intelligence and real human behavior.

I create calm, low-friction digital experiences that reduce overwhelm and support how people actually think, feel, and move through tasks. My work blends softness with structure, balancing intuitive design with systems that are clear, accessible, and build-ready.

I’m especially interested in neurodivergent UX, where traditional patterns like streaks, gamification, and dense interfaces often miss the mark. I design flexible, low-pressure interactions that feel easier to return to and better reflect how people actually operate.

My perspective is intentional. My approach is human-first. And my work lives at the intersection of feeling and function.
